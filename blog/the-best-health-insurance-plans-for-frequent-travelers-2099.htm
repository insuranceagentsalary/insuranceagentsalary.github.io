The Best Health Insurance Plans for Frequent Travelers

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Traveling frequently is an exciting lifestyle, but it comes with unique challenges—especially when it comes to healthcare. Whether you're a digital nomad, an expat, or a business traveler, having the right health insurance is non-negotiable. Unlike standard plans, travel-friendly health insurance must cover international emergencies, telemedicine, and unexpected medical evacuations.

In this guide, we’ll break down the top health insurance options for frequent travelers, what to look for in a plan, and how to avoid costly surprises.

Why Standard Health Insurance Isn’t Enough

Most domestic health insurance plans have limited or no coverage outside your home country. Even if they offer some international benefits, they often exclude:

  • Emergency evacuations – A medical flight can cost over $100,000.
  • Pre-existing conditions – Many insurers won’t cover these abroad.
  • Local healthcare networks – Without partnerships, you might pay out-of-pocket upfront.

For frequent travelers, specialized insurance is a must.

Top Health Insurance Plans for Frequent Travelers

1. Cigna Global

Best for: Expats and long-term travelers

Cigna Global offers customizable plans with worldwide coverage, including the U.S. (if selected). Key features:

  • 24/7 multilingual support
  • Direct billing with international hospitals
  • Optional dental and vision add-ons

Drawback: Premiums can be high for older travelers.

2. GeoBlue Xplorer

Best for: U.S. citizens traveling frequently

GeoBlue partners with Blue Cross Blue Shield, offering strong U.S. coverage plus international benefits:

  • Access to Blue Cross network abroad
  • Emergency evacuation included
  • Telehealth services

Drawback: Requires a U.S.-based primary health plan.

3. SafetyWing Nomad Insurance

Best for: Digital nomads and budget travelers

SafetyWing is designed for remote workers with flexible, subscription-based plans:

  • Covers 180+ countries
  • $250,000 max per incident
  • Includes limited COVID-19 coverage

Drawback: Lower coverage limits than premium plans.

4. Allianz Worldwide Care

Best for: Business travelers and families

Allianz provides comprehensive global coverage with:

  • Routine and emergency care
  • Mental health support
  • Family plans available

Drawback: Requires medical underwriting for pre-existing conditions.

5. IMG Patriot Travel Medical Insurance

Best for: Short-term trips (1 year or less)

IMG’s Patriot plans are affordable and flexible:

  • Coverage from 5 days to 1 year
  • Optional adventure sports rider
  • Renewable online

Drawback: Not ideal for long-term expats.

Key Features to Look For

Global Coverage

Ensure your plan covers at least the regions you frequent. Some exclude high-risk countries.

Emergency Evacuation & Repatriation

This is critical if you’re in remote areas or unstable regions.

Pre-Existing Conditions

Few insurers cover these, but some (like Cigna) offer partial coverage with medical screening.

Telemedicine

Virtual doctor visits can save time and hassle when traveling.

Flexible Payment Options

Monthly subscriptions (like SafetyWing) work better for nomads than annual contracts.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Assuming credit card travel insurance is enough – Most cap payouts at $50,000.
  • Overlooking policy exclusions – Adventure sports, pandemics, or war zones may not be covered.
  • Not checking local healthcare quality – In some countries, medical evacuation is a necessity.

Final Tips for Choosing a Plan

  1. Assess your travel style – Backpackers need different coverage than corporate travelers.
  2. Compare deductibles and co-pays – A low premium might mean high out-of-pocket costs.
  3. Read reviews – Look for feedback on claim processing speed and customer service.

With the right plan, you can explore the world without worrying about medical emergencies. Safe travels!
