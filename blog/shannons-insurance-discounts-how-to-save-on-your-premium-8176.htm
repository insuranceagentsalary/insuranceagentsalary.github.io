Shannons Insurance Discounts: How to Save on Your Premium

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Let’s be honest: insurance isn’t exactly the most thrilling topic in the world. But saving money? That’s something we can all get excited about. If you’re a car enthusiast, a collector of classic vehicles, or simply someone who owns a unique ride, you’ve probably heard of Shannons Insurance. They’ve built a legendary reputation for understanding the passion behind special cars, motorcycles, and even vintage caravans. But what many people don’t realize is that beyond their tailored coverage, Shannons offers a variety of ways to make your premium more affordable. In today’s economic climate, where every dollar counts amidst global supply chain issues and rising inflation, unlocking these discounts isn’t just a nice-to-have—it’s essential.

This isn’t about finding a cut-rate policy that leaves you exposed. It’s about working with a specialist insurer to get the value you deserve without compromising on the quality of your coverage. We’re going to dive deep into the world of Shannons insurance discounts, exploring not just the obvious savings but the strategic moves you can make to protect your passion and your wallet.

Understanding the Shannons Philosophy: It’s Not Just a Policy, It’s a Partnership

Before we get into the specific discounts, it’s crucial to understand what makes Shannons different. Unlike mainstream insurers who often see your vehicle as just a set of numbers (make, model, year), Shannons sees the story. They understand that your meticulously restored 1971 Holden Monaro or your perfectly maintained 1990s Japanese import isn’t just a mode of transport; it’s a part of your identity.

This philosophy is the foundation for their approach to pricing and discounts. Their premiums are already competitively structured for enthusiast vehicles, but they reward behavior that aligns with lower risk and a genuine passion for the community. They’re not just looking for customers; they’re looking for partners in preserving automotive history.

How Global Events Are Reshaping Insurance Costs

You might wonder why your premium fluctuates. It’s not just about your driving record. In our interconnected world, macro events have a direct impact. The global semiconductor chip shortage, a lingering effect of the COVID-19 pandemic, has crippled new car production. This has caused a massive spike in the value of used and classic cars. A car that was worth $30,000 two years ago might be valued at $45,000 today. For an insurer, this means the potential payout in a total loss claim is significantly higher, which in turn affects premiums across the board.

Furthermore, climate change is increasing the frequency and severity of extreme weather events. More hailstorms, floods, and bushfires mean insurers are paying out more claims than ever before. These costs are inevitably distributed across the insurance pool. Understanding this context is key—it highlights why proactively seeking discounts is a smart financial move in today’s volatile world.

A Deep Dive into Shannons Insurance Discount Strategies

Now, let’s get to the good stuff. How can you actually save money on your Shannons premium? The discounts generally fall into a few key categories.

1. The Multi-Policy Discount (The Bundle)

This is one of the most straightforward and effective ways to save. If you have more than one vehicle that qualifies for Shannons coverage—say, your daily driver and a weekend project car—insuring them both under the same Shannons policy can lead to significant savings. But it doesn’t stop there. Shannons also offers insurance for motorcycles, caravans, and even home insurance for eligible customers. Bundling your car and your home, or your car and your bike, can unlock a generous multi-policy discount. It’s a win-win: you simplify your paperwork and save money.

2. The Security Discount

Protecting your prized possession is a top priority, and Shannons will reward you for it. The more secure your vehicle is, the less likely it is to be stolen or vandalized, which lowers the risk for the insurer. You can typically get discounts for: * Garaging: Keeping your vehicle locked in a secure, private garage overnight is a huge risk reducer. * Alarms and Immobilizers: Having a Australian Standards-approved alarm and immobilizer system professionally installed is a proven theft deterrent. * Tracking Devices: For high-value vehicles, a GPS tracking device can not only help recover the car if stolen but also often qualifies for a premium discount.

When you apply for a quote, be prepared to provide details about your storage and security arrangements. The investment in good security pays for itself both in peace of mind and insurance savings.

3. The Driver Profile Discount

Who drives the car and how it’s driven are critical factors. Shannons specializes in insurance for enthusiasts, and they understand that a classic car is often used for pleasure rather than a daily commute. * Limited Use/Low Mileage: If you drive your vehicle less than a certain number of kilometers per year (e.g., 7,500 km or 4,000 km), you should definitely declare this. Less time on the road directly correlates to a lower chance of an accident. * Mature Age Drivers: Statistically, experienced drivers are involved in fewer accidents. If the primary drivers on the policy are over a certain age, this can work in your favor. * Good Driving History: A clean driving record, free of at-fault claims and serious traffic violations, is your most valuable asset when seeking affordable insurance. It demonstrates responsibility and low risk.

4. The Club Membership Discount

This is a discount that truly speaks to the Shannons ethos. Being an active member of a recognized car club or enthusiast association shows that you are part of a community that values and cares for vehicles. Club members often share knowledge about maintenance, security, and safe driving practices, making them lower-risk policyholders. Always inform Shannons of your club affiliations—it’s one of the easiest ways to prove your enthusiast status and get a discount for it.

5. The Loyalty and Upfront Payment Discount

Sticking with Shannons year after year can have its perks. While not always advertised, loyalty is often valued. Furthermore, opting to pay your annual premium in one lump sum rather than in monthly installments can sometimes help you avoid additional installment fees, effectively reducing your total cost.

Proactive Measures: Beyond the Discount List

Saving money with Shannons isn’t just about asking for discounts; it’s about structuring your policy intelligently.

Agreed Value vs. Market Value: Choosing the Right Coverage

This is perhaps the most important decision for an enthusiast. A Market Value policy pays out what the car is deemed to be worth at the time of the claim, which can be a contentious and often disappointing process. An Agreed Value policy, a hallmark of Shannons, means you and the insurer agree on the car’s value upfront and that is the amount paid in a total loss (minus any excess). While an Agreed Value policy might have a slightly higher premium, it provides absolute certainty. The savings come from avoiding the devastating financial loss of an underinsured claim. It’s not a direct discount, but it is a critical form of financial protection and value.

Adjusting Your Excess

Your excess is the amount you agree to pay towards any claim. Opting for a higher voluntary excess can lower your premium. This is a strategic gamble: if you are a very safe driver with a secure garage and a low likelihood of claiming, a higher excess can save you money year after year. Just be sure you can comfortably afford to pay that excess amount if you ever need to make a claim.

The Annual Review Ritual

The single biggest mistake policyholders make is letting their insurance auto-renew without a second thought. Your circumstances change, and so does the value of your vehicle. Once a year, before your renewal notice arrives, do this: 1. Reassess Your Agreed Value: Is it still accurate? With market values soaring, you may need to increase it to avoid being underinsured. 2. Audit Your Discounts: Have you installed a new alarm? Joined a new club? Driven even fewer kilometers? Call Shannons and make sure your policy reflects your current situation. 3. Ask the Question: Simply having a conversation with a Shannons representative can uncover new savings opportunities. They can review your policy and ensure you’re getting all the discounts you’re eligible for.

The goal is to shift your mindset from being a passive payer to an active manager of your insurance portfolio. In a world of economic uncertainty, taking control of these recurring costs is a powerful way to manage your budget without sacrificing the things you love. Your passion deserves the best protection, and with a few smart strategies, it can be more affordable than you think.
