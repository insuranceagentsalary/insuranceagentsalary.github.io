How to Find DUI-Friendly Car Insurance Companies

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Getting a DUI (Driving Under the Influence) conviction can drastically change your life—especially when it comes to car insurance. Many insurers see drivers with DUIs as high-risk, leading to skyrocketing premiums or even policy cancellations. However, not all hope is lost. Some companies specialize in offering DUI-friendly car insurance, providing coverage even after a conviction.

In this guide, we’ll explore how to find insurers willing to work with DUI offenders, what factors affect your rates, and strategies to secure the best possible deal.

Understanding DUI and Its Impact on Car Insurance

A DUI conviction stays on your record for years, affecting everything from employment opportunities to insurance costs. Insurance companies assess risk based on driving history, and a DUI signals reckless behavior, making you a liability in their eyes.

How Long Does a DUI Affect Insurance Rates?

Most states report DUIs to insurance companies for 3 to 10 years, depending on local laws. During this time, you’ll likely be classified as a high-risk driver, meaning:
- Higher premiums
- Limited policy options
- Possible requirement for an SR-22 or FR-44 (proof of financial responsibility)

Why Some Insurers Are More DUI-Friendly Than Others

Not all insurers penalize DUI offenders equally. Some specialize in non-standard auto insurance, catering to high-risk drivers. These companies may offer:
- More flexible underwriting
- Lower rate increases compared to mainstream insurers
- Willingness to work with multiple violations

Steps to Finding DUI-Friendly Car Insurance

1. Compare Multiple Quotes

Never settle for the first offer. Use comparison tools or work with an independent insurance agent who can shop around for you. Some insurers known for being DUI-friendly include:
- The General
- Dairyland
- Progressive
- SafeAuto

2. Look for Non-Standard Insurance Providers

Mainstream insurers like State Farm or Allstate may drop you after a DUI. Instead, focus on companies that specialize in high-risk drivers.

3. Check for SR-22/FR-44 Filing Services

After a DUI, many states require an SR-22 or FR-44 form to prove you carry the minimum required insurance. Not all insurers file these, so confirm before purchasing a policy.

4. Consider Usage-Based Insurance

If your driving habits improve post-DUI, usage-based insurance (UBI) programs (like Progressive’s Snapshot) may help lower rates over time by tracking safe driving behavior.

5. Ask About Discounts

Even with a DUI, you may qualify for discounts such as:
- Defensive driving course completion
- Multi-policy bundling
- Good student discounts (if applicable)

Factors That Affect Your DUI Insurance Rates

State Laws and Penalties

Insurance costs vary by state. For example:
- California requires DUIs to stay on record for 10 years.
- Florida mandates an FR-44, doubling minimum liability coverage.

Prior Driving Record

A clean record before the DUI may help mitigate rate hikes, while multiple violations will worsen premiums.

Type of Vehicle

Insuring a luxury car or sports vehicle after a DUI will be far more expensive than a standard sedan.

Credit Score

In most states, insurers use credit-based insurance scores. Improving your credit can help lower premiums.

Alternative Options If Traditional Insurance Is Too Expensive

State-Assigned Risk Pools

If no insurer will cover you, your state’s assigned risk pool (like California’s CAARP) provides mandatory coverage—though at high rates.

Named Driver Policies

If you’re not the primary driver, being listed on someone else’s policy (like a family member’s) may be cheaper—though this isn’t always legal or advisable.

Public Transportation or Rideshare

If insurance is unaffordable, consider alternative transportation until your record improves.

Long-Term Strategies to Lower Insurance Costs After a DUI

Maintain a Clean Driving Record

Avoid any further violations to show insurers you’re a lower risk over time.

Take a Defensive Driving Course

Some insurers offer discounts for completing approved courses.

Increase Your Deductible

Opting for a higher deductible can reduce monthly premiums—just ensure you can afford the out-of-pocket cost if an accident occurs.

Re-Shop Your Policy Annually

As your DUI ages, better rates may become available. Don’t assume your current insurer offers the best deal forever.

Final Thoughts

A DUI doesn’t have to mean financial ruin. By researching DUI-friendly insurers, comparing quotes, and improving your driving habits, you can find affordable coverage and rebuild your record over time. Stay proactive, and don’t hesitate to seek help from insurance professionals who specialize in high-risk cases.
