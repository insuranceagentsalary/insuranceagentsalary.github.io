What Does Progressive Full Coverage Auto Insurance Really Include?

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

You’ve heard the term a thousand times, probably from a salesperson, a friend, or while reviewing your own policy: “You should really get full coverage.” It sounds definitive, like a forcefield around your car, protecting you from every possible financial misfortune on the road. But here’s the industry secret that isn’t much of a secret to those in the know: there is no single, officially defined insurance product called “Full Coverage.”

Instead, “full coverage” is a colloquial, convenient shorthand for a robust combination of core auto insurance coverages that, when bundled together, provide a much higher level of financial protection than the bare-minimum liability insurance required by state laws. In today’s world—marked by supply chain disruptions, soaring repair costs due to advanced technology, and increasing climate-related disasters—understanding the true components of this so-called full coverage isn’t just wise; it’s essential for your financial security.

Deconstructing the "Full Coverage" Bundle

At its heart, a robust full coverage policy from a provider like Progressive is built on three fundamental pillars of protection, going far beyond just covering the damage you might cause to others.

1. Liability Insurance: The Non-Negotiable Foundation

This is the only part of your insurance that is legally mandated in most states. It doesn't cover your car at all; it covers your legal responsibility for bodily injury and property damage you cause to others in an accident.

  • Bodily Injury Liability (BI): If you are at fault in an accident, this pays for the medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering of the other driver and their passengers. In our litigious society, having high BI limits is crucial. A serious injury can lead to medical bills that soar into the hundreds of thousands of dollars or more.
  • Property Damage Liability (PD): This covers the cost of repairing or replacing the other driver’s vehicle, as well as other property you might damage, like a fence, a building, or an expensive streetlamp.

While liability is the legal baseline, a true full coverage policy layers on protections for you and your vehicle.

2. Collision Coverage: Protecting Your Investment

As the name implies, collision coverage pays for damage to your own car resulting from—you guessed it—a collision. It doesn’t matter if you hit another car, a tree, a guardrail, or a pothole. If your car is damaged from an impact, this coverage kicks in, minus your deductible. In an era of persistent supply chain issues, where a simple bumper replacement can take months and cost a fortune due to part shortages, this coverage is more critical than ever.

3. Comprehensive Coverage: The "Acts of God (and Everything Else)" Protector

If collision covers crashes, comprehensive covers almost everything else that can happen to your car while it's not moving. This is the part of your policy that has become increasingly vital due to several modern global challenges.

  • Climate Change and Extreme Weather: From hailstorms the size of baseballs and catastrophic flooding to wildfires and falling trees, comprehensive coverage is your financial shield against the growing frequency and intensity of weather-related events.
  • Theft and Vandalism: With the rise in organized vehicle theft, particularly of certain SUV and pickup truck models, this coverage is indispensable. It also covers "smash-and-grab" break-ins and malicious keying of your paint.
  • Animal Collisions: Hitting a deer, elk, or other large animal is a surprisingly common and damaging event, covered under comprehensive, not collision.
  • Falling Objects and Other Surprises: This can include anything from a tree branch in a storm to a collapsed garage roof.

Beyond the Big Three: The Essential Add-Ons for Modern Driving

Calling a policy with just liability, collision, and comprehensive "full coverage" is like calling a basic smartphone "fully featured" without any apps. To truly be comprehensive in today's driving landscape, you need to consider these powerful add-ons, many of which Progressive offers and are often included in their most robust bundles.

Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage (UM/UIM)

This is arguably one of the most important coverages you can have. Shockingly, despite it being illegal, millions of drivers in the U.S. operate vehicles with no insurance at all. Many more carry only the state minimums, which are often woefully inadequate. If you're hit by one of these drivers, UM/UIM protects you.

  • UM Bodily Injury: Pays for your and your passengers' medical bills, lost wages, and pain and suffering.
  • UM Property Damage: Covers the repair costs for your vehicle.

Given the number of uninsured drivers on the road, skipping this coverage is a significant financial gamble.

Medical Payments (MedPay) or Personal Injury Protection (PIP)

Often referred to as "no-fault" coverage, these pay for medical expenses for you and your passengers, regardless of who caused the accident. PIP is broader than MedPay and can also cover related costs like lost income, essential services (e.g., child care if you're incapacitated), and even funeral expenses. With healthcare costs being a top concern for many Americans, this coverage provides a crucial layer of immediate financial assistance after a crash.

The Game Changers: Rental Reimbursement and Roadside Assistance

The real-world inconvenience of an accident is a major modern stressor.

  • Rental Reimbursement: With the average repair time stretching due to complex car technology and part delays, this coverage pays for a rental car while your vehicle is in the shop. Without it, you could be facing weeks or months of out-of-pocket transportation costs.
  • Roadside Assistance: A dead battery in an electric vehicle, a flat tire on a highway, or simply locking your keys in a keyless-entry car—Progressive’s roadside assistance provides towing, jump-starts, tire changes, and lockout services. It’s peace of mind in a membership.

Full Coverage in the Age of High-Tech Vehicles

The definition of a "repair" has been fundamentally transformed. A modern car is a supercomputer on wheels, and this reality directly impacts your full coverage policy.

The Sensor and Camera Dilemma

A minor fender-bender that once cost a few hundred dollars to fix can now run into thousands. Why? Because even a low-speed impact can damage sensors for automatic emergency braking, radar for adaptive cruise control, and cameras for lane-keeping assistance. Calibrating these systems after a repair requires specialized equipment and certified technicians, dramatically increasing the cost. A true full coverage policy must account for this, and insurers like Progressive adjust their claims processes and repair network to handle these high-tech repairs.

Electric Vehicles (EVs) and Specialized Repair

Insuring an EV introduces new considerations. The battery pack, often the most expensive component of the vehicle, can be easily damaged in an undercarriage impact. Repairing it is not a simple task and requires specific expertise. Furthermore, parts for EVs can be even more scarce. Your full coverage policy needs the financial backing and repair network capable of managing these unique and expensive challenges.

How to Customize Your "Full Coverage" with Progressive

There is no one-size-fits-all answer. Building your optimal full coverage package depends on your personal circumstances.

  • Assess Your Vehicle's Value: If your car is older and its value is low, carrying collision and comprehensive with a low deductible might not be cost-effective. The potential payout might be less than the premiums you pay over time.
  • Choose Your Deductibles Wisely: Your deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket before your insurance kicks in. Higher deductibles (e.g., $1,000) lower your premium but mean more upfront cost in a claim. Lower deductibles (e.g., $250) mean higher premiums but less financial stress when you need to file a claim.
  • Evaluate Your Financial Exposure: Don’t just buy the state minimum liability limits. If you have significant assets—a home, savings, investments—you need liability limits high enough to protect them from a lawsuit. Consider an Umbrella Policy for an extra layer of liability protection beyond your auto insurance limits.
  • Utilize Progressive's Tools: Progressive offers features like the Name Your Price® tool and bundling discounts (for also having your home or renters insurance with them). They also offer the Snapshot® usage-based program, which can potentially lower your premium based on your safe driving habits.

Ultimately, a truly "full" coverage auto insurance policy is a dynamic, customized shield. It’s a package that starts with the foundational trio of liability, collision, and comprehensive and is then fortified with UM/UIM, medical coverage, and convenience services like rental and roadside assistance. In a world of complex cars, unpredictable weather, and financial uncertainty, it’s the closest you can get to that promised forcefield, ensuring that a single accident or a rogue hailstorm doesn’t derail your financial future.
