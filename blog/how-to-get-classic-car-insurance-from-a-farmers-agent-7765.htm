How to Get Classic Car Insurance from a Farmers Agent

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

In an era defined by rapid technological advancement and a collective push toward sustainability, there’s a beautiful, rumbling counter-culture thriving on our roads: the world of classic car ownership. These rolling pieces of art represent more than just transportation; they are touchstones to history, craftsmanship, and a simpler, analog time. Yet, in our complex modern world of climate concerns, economic fluctuations, and digital everything, protecting these cherished machines requires a specialized approach. A standard auto policy simply won’t do justice to the value—both sentimental and monetary—of your 1965 Mustang or 1957 Bel Air.

This is where the expertise of a Farmers Insurance agent becomes invaluable. Partnering with a local Farmers agent to secure classic car insurance isn’t just a transaction; it’s about forming a relationship with a professional who understands that you’re not just insuring a car, you’re preserving a legacy. This guide will walk you through the entire process, highlighting why this partnership is crucial in today’s world.

Why Classic Car Insurance is a Different Beast

Think of your classic car. Now think of your daily driver. They are fundamentally different, and their insurance needs reflect that. Standard insurance policies are based on the concept of depreciating value—your car loses value the moment you drive it off the lot and continues to do so each year. Classic cars, however, often appreciate in value. They are also driven less frequently, stored more carefully, and maintained with a focus on originality or period-correct parts.

The "Agreed Value" Advantage

This is the cornerstone of classic car insurance. Unlike a standard policy that might only pay out the "actual cash value" (a depreciated value) after a total loss, an "Agreed Value" policy means you and your Farmers agent agree upon the car's value upfront, backed by appraisals, photos, and market research. If the worst happens, that is the amount you will be paid, no questions asked. This provides immense peace of mind in a volatile economic climate where classic car values can be unpredictable.

Understanding Usage Restrictions

Classic car insurance isn’t for your daily commute. Policies are designed for vehicles used for pleasure—like car shows, parades, weekend drives, and occasional exhibitions. This limited usage is a key reason premiums are often significantly lower than those for standard vehicles, despite the higher coverage limits. Your Farmers agent will help you clearly define and understand these usage parameters to ensure you're never unknowingly at risk.

Navigating Modern Challenges with a Classic Mindset

The world of classic car ownership is not immune to 21st-century issues. A good insurance policy and a knowledgeable agent help you navigate these new realities.

Climate Change and Severe Weather Threats

With increasing frequency of severe weather events—hurricanes, wildfires, catastrophic hail storms—where and how you store your vehicle is more important than ever. A Farmers agent will stress the necessity of a secure, climate-controlled garage. Your policy’s comprehensive coverage is what protects against these "acts of God," and your agent can advise on specific steps to mitigate risk, potentially preventing a claim before disaster strikes.

The Global Supply Chain and Parts Sourcing

The pandemic highlighted the fragility of global supply chains. Sourcing a specific, original fender for a 1960s European sports car can now take months, if it's available at all. A quality classic car policy from Farmers often includes provisions for original manufacturer (OEM) or even rare, hand-fabricated parts, ensuring your restoration isn’t stalled or compromised by a lack of available components.

The Digital Valuation Landscape

The value of classic cars is increasingly set in a global, digital marketplace. Online auction results from sites like Bring a Trailer instantly influence market values. An experienced Farmers agent stays attuned to these trends, ensuring your Agreed Value reflects the true, current market rate, protecting your investment from being underinsured.

A Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Covered with Farmers

Securing your classic car insurance through a Farmers agent is a straightforward, personalized process. Here’s how to prepare for that conversation and what to expect.

Step 1: Do Your Homework Before You Call

Gather all relevant information about your vehicle. This includes:

  • Vehicle Identification Number (VIN): The unique 17-digit code for your car.
  • Full History: Documentation of ownership history, restoration work, and any past awards.
  • Photographic Evidence: A current set of high-quality photos from all angles, inside and out, including the engine bay and trunk.
  • Appraisal Documents: If you have had a recent professional appraisal, have it ready.
  • Odometer Reading: An accurate note of the mileage.
  • Details on Storage: Where is the car kept? Is it a private garage, a shared storage unit, a climate-controlled facility? Details matter.

Step 2: Find and Consult with Your Local Farmers Agent

This is the most critical step. Don't just buy online; build a relationship. Use the Farmers website to locate a well-reviewed agent near you. Schedule a meeting, either in person or over the phone, specifically to discuss classic car insurance. Come with your prepared documentation and be ready to talk about your car with passion—it helps the agent understand what they’re helping to protect.

Step 3: The Qualification Conversation

Your agent will ask a series of questions to determine if your car and your situation qualify for classic coverage. Typical criteria include:

  • Vehicle Age: Most companies require a car to be at least 25 years old, but some "modern classics" or special interest vehicles may qualify sooner.
  • Driver’s Record: A clean driving history is typically required for all drivers in the household.
  • Primary Vehicle: You must have a regular, modern vehicle for daily use.
  • Storage: You must have a secure, enclosed garage for storage.
  • Usage Limits: You must agree to mileage limits, usually between 1,000 and 6,000 miles per year.

Step 4: Determining the "Agreed Value"

This is a collaborative process. Using your photos, appraisals, and their knowledge of the current market, you and your agent will arrive at a fair value for the vehicle. Be prepared to justify your number with recent sales of comparable models. This is not the time to be sentimental; it’s the time to be factual and ensure your investment is fully protected.

Step 5: Reviewing the Policy specifics

Your Farmers agent will walk you through the entire policy. Pay close attention to:

  • Coverage Types: Liability, Comprehensive, Collision, Uninsured Motorist.
  • Deductibles: The amount you pay out-of-pocket in the event of a claim.
  • Special Coverages: Are spare parts, tools, and memorabilia covered? What about coverage during a cross-country road trip for a show?
  • Claims Process: Understand exactly what to do and who to call if you need to file a claim. A good agent will be your advocate.

Step 6: Finalizing the Policy and Hitting the Road

Once you are comfortable with the terms and the price, you’ll finalize the policy. Keep a copy of the Agreed Value declaration and the policy documents with your important car records. Then, you can enjoy the open road with the confidence that your piece of history is protected by a policy and an agent that truly understands its worth.

Beyond the Policy: Your Farmers Agent as a Risk Management Partner

The relationship with your agent shouldn’t end once the policy is signed. The best agents act as ongoing partners in risk management. They can be a resource for recommending secure storage facilities, reputable repair shops that specialize in classics, and even safe driving routes for your cherished vehicle. As your collection grows or the market changes, an annual review with your agent ensures your coverage never falls behind. In a world of constant change, that kind of steadfast, personalized service is the ultimate classic.
