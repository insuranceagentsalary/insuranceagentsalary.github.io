Dental Accidents in Pets: How to Get Insurance Coverage

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Pets are more than just animals—they’re family. And just like any family member, their health and well-being are a top priority. One often overlooked aspect of pet care is dental health. Dental accidents in pets, such as broken teeth, gum disease, or oral infections, can be painful, expensive, and sometimes life-threatening. With veterinary costs rising and pet insurance becoming increasingly popular, understanding how to secure coverage for dental accidents is crucial for responsible pet owners.

Why Pet Dental Health Matters

Dental issues in pets are more common than many owners realize. According to the American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA), 80% of dogs and 70% of cats show signs of oral disease by age three. Left untreated, dental problems can lead to severe pain, difficulty eating, and even systemic infections affecting the heart, liver, and kidneys.

Common Dental Accidents in Pets

  1. Broken or Fractured Teeth – Often caused by chewing on hard objects like bones or rocks.
  2. Gum Disease (Periodontal Disease) – A leading cause of tooth loss in older pets.
  3. Oral Trauma – Accidents like falls or fights with other animals can damage teeth and gums.
  4. Tooth Abscesses – Infections that can spread if not treated promptly.

The Rising Cost of Pet Dental Care

Veterinary dental procedures can be expensive, especially in emergencies. A simple tooth extraction can cost $500–$1,000, while advanced treatments like root canals or oral surgery can exceed $3,000. Many pet owners are unprepared for these unexpected expenses, leading to difficult financial decisions.

How Pet Insurance Can Help

Pet insurance policies vary, but many now include dental coverage—either as part of a standard plan or as an add-on. Here’s what to look for when choosing a policy:

1. Accident-Only vs. Comprehensive Coverage

  • Accident-only plans may cover sudden dental injuries (e.g., a broken tooth from chewing).
  • Comprehensive plans often include preventive care (cleanings) and treatment for dental diseases.

2. Coverage Limits and Exclusions

  • Some insurers cap dental benefits (e.g., $1,000 per year).
  • Pre-existing conditions are typically excluded.

3. Waiting Periods

  • Most policies have a waiting period (e.g., 14–30 days) before dental coverage kicks in.

Top Pet Insurance Providers with Dental Coverage

Here are some leading companies offering dental accident coverage:

1. Healthy Paws

  • Covers dental injuries (e.g., broken teeth) but not routine cleanings.
  • No annual or lifetime payout limits.

2. Nationwide

  • Offers Whole Pet with Wellness, covering accidents, illnesses, and preventive dental care.
  • Higher premiums but more comprehensive coverage.

3. Trupanion

  • Covers dental injuries and diseases if not pre-existing.
  • Direct payment to vets in some cases.

4. Embrace Pet Insurance

  • Optional Wellness Rewards add-on for dental cleanings.
  • Diminishing deductible feature.

How to File a Claim for a Pet Dental Accident

  1. Visit a Licensed Veterinarian – Ensure the vet provides a detailed report of the dental issue.
  2. Check Your Policy – Confirm coverage limits and exclusions.
  3. Submit Documentation – Include invoices, medical records, and photos if applicable.
  4. Follow Up – Some insurers require additional forms or vet statements.

Preventing Dental Emergencies

While insurance helps with costs, prevention is always better than cure. Here’s how to protect your pet’s teeth:

1. Regular Brushing

  • Use pet-safe toothpaste and brushes.

2. Dental Chews and Toys

  • Look for Veterinary Oral Health Council (VOHC)-approved products.

3. Annual Vet Checkups

  • Professional cleanings can catch problems early.

4. Avoid Hard Chews

  • Bones, antlers, and hard plastic toys can fracture teeth.

Final Thoughts

Dental accidents in pets are a real and often expensive concern. With the right pet insurance, you can ensure your furry friend gets the care they need without breaking the bank. Research policies carefully, prioritize preventive care, and always consult your vet for the best dental health practices.

By staying informed and proactive, you can keep your pet’s smile healthy—and your wallet happy.
